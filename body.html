The truth about your paint

What's hidingbeneath the surface.

Swirl Marks

Every automated car wash, every dry wipe, every untrained hand leaves circular scratches in your clearcoat. Under direct light, your paint looks like a spiderweb. Most owners don't notice until someone shows them.

Affects 95% of vehicles over 1 year old

Water Spots

Mineral deposits from hard water, sprinklers, and rain etch into your clearcoat over time. Left untreated, they bond permanently — no amount of washing removes them. The Inland Empire's hard water makes this worse.

Permanent etching begins within 48 hours

Oxidation

Southern California's UV exposure is relentless. Without protection, your clearcoat breaks down molecule by molecule — paint fades, loses depth, turns chalky. The sun doesn't stop working on your paint just because you parked in shade.

UV damage accelerates 3x without coating protection

Road Damage

The 10, the 60, the 71 — every mile on IE freeways launches micro-debris at your paint. Rock chips, tar, brake dust, industrial fallout. Each one is a breach in your clearcoat that spreads if left unaddressed.

Average vehicle sustains 200+ micro-impacts per year
